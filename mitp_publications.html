MITP Publications 2025

Below is a selection of research publications associated with the Mandelstam Institute for Theoretical Physics (MITP) in 2025. These works span a broad range of topics across theoretical and mathematical physics, condensed matter physics, and applied thermal engineering.

Urn models, Markov chains and random walks in cosmological topologically massive gravity at the critical point
Yannick Mvondo She
Journal of Geometry and Physics, Vol. 207 (2025), Article 105347
This work explores stochastic processes such as urn models and random walks within the framework of cosmological topologically massive gravity. By focusing on the critical point of the theory, the paper reveals novel connections between probabilistic dynamics and gravitational models in lower dimensions.

DOI: 10.1016/j.geomphys.2024.1053487
Existence of a flat band in highly correlated metal BaCo2P2
Arthur van der Spuy, Robert Warmbier, Abhishek Pandey
Journal of Physics: Condensed Matter, Vol. 37 (2025), Article 155602
This paper investigates the electronic structure of the strongly correlated metal BaCo2P2, demonstrating the existence of a flat electronic band. Such features are of particular interest due to their connection with enhanced correlation effects and emergent quantum phases.

DOI: 10.1088/1361-648X/adb675
Suppression of ferromagnetic correlations in hole-doped SrCo2As2
Mahlogonolo Morena, Arthur van der Spuy, Y. Liu, Robert Warmbier, Abhishek Pandey
Journal of Physics: Materials, Vol. 8 (2025), Article 025009
The authors study the impact of hole doping on magnetic correlations in SrCo2As2. Their results show a suppression of ferromagnetic tendencies, providing insight into how electronic tuning influences magnetic behavior in correlated materials.

DOI: 10.1088/2515-7639/adb801
Validated theoretical model for IR-reflective heat transfer in cylindrical annuli: experiment, simulation, and application
Khaled Mohamad, Philippe Ferrer
Applied Thermal Engineering, Vol. 278 (2025), Article 127171
This work presents a validated theoretical model for infrared-reflective heat transfer in cylindrical annular geometries. Combining experimental measurements with numerical simulations, the study demonstrates the accuracy and practical applicability of the model in engineering contexts.

DOI: 10.1016/j.applthermaleng.2025.127171
