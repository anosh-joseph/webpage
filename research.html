Research

My research focuses on non-perturbative aspects of quantum field theory, supersymmetric gauge theories, and computational approaches to strongly coupled systems. I combine analytical insights with large-scale numerical simulations to study quantum field theories beyond the reach of perturbation theory.

Research Areas

My core research interests lie at the interface of high-energy theory and computational physics, with emphasis on:

  • Lattice formulations of supersymmetric quantum field theories
  • Matrix models and random matrix theory
  • Non-perturbative dynamics of gauge theories and quantum mechanics
  • Monte Carlo methods, including Complex Langevin dynamics
  • Sign problems and numerical instabilities in quantum field theory

A central theme of my work is the development and application of numerical tools that preserve symmetries while enabling controlled access to strongly coupled regimes.

Current Projects

  • Complex Langevin simulations of supersymmetric theories: Developing and applying Complex Langevin methods to study supersymmetric quantum mechanics, lattice field theories, and matrix models with complex actions.
  • Matrix quantum mechanics and holography: Non-perturbative lattice studies of BFSS and BMN-type matrix models, focusing on thermal phase structure and connections to gauge–gravity duality.
  • Lattice supersymmetry: Investigations of supersymmetric Yang–Mills theories using lattice formulations that preserve a subset of exact supersymmetry at finite lattice spacing.
  • High-performance scientific computing: Development of efficient simulation frameworks for large-N systems and extensive Monte Carlo calculations.

Past Projects

  • Lattice studies of two-dimensional and four-dimensional supersymmetric Yang–Mills theories, including investigations of phase structure and supersymmetry breaking.
  • Early numerical studies of sign problems in lattice supersymmetry and strategies to mitigate them using deformation techniques.
  • Tensor network and renormalization-group approaches to classical and quantum spin systems.
  • Non-perturbative investigations of matrix models relevant to string theory and quantum gravity.
